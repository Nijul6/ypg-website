Our Mission

At the Pharmaceutical Society of Nigeria - Young Pharmacists Group (PSN-YPG), we are committed to empowering young Pharmacists to become leaders and catalysts for positive change in the profession and society. Through collaboration, innovation, and professional excellence, we equip individuals to drive impact at local, national, and global levels, ensuring sustainable development and progress in healthcare.

Our Vision

1. We envision a future where young Pharmacists play a pivotal role in advancing the nation’s health, leveraging their expertise to improve patient care and public health outcomes.
2. We are dedicated to ensuring that Pharmacy remains a cornerstone of healthcare, continuously evolving to meet the needs of a dynamic health system.

Pharmaceutical Society of Nigeria – Young Pharmacists Group (PSN-YPG)

Brief History

The Pharmaceutical Society of Nigeria - Young Pharmacists Group (PSN-YPG) is an interest group within the Pharmaceutical Society of Nigeria (PSN), dedicated to fostering the growth and development of young Pharmacists.
Membership is open to all Pharmacists licensed by the Pharmacists Council of Nigeria (PCN) who are 35 years old or younger and/or within five years post-graduation from an accredited Pharmacy school.

PSN-YPG was founded in 2012 as an evolution of the Young Pharmacists' Forum (YPF), aligning with the global model of the Early Career Pharmacists’ Group (ECPG) of the International Pharmaceutical Federation (FIP). It was created to empower early-career Pharmacists, bridge generational gaps in leadership, and amplify the collective voice of young professionals in shaping the future of Pharmacy in Nigeria.

At its core, PSN-YPG thrives on the synergy of innovation, intellectual capacity, and youthful energy. Beyond professional development, the Group is committed to driving positive change within the Pharmacy profession and society at large. Through continuous learning, leadership grooming, and strategic collaborations, PSN-YPG continues to redefine the role of young Pharmacists—transforming them into change makers, thought leaders, and key contributors to national and global healthcare systems.
